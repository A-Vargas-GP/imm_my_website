My Website Critiques

Sites I Love to Use and Reasons Why:

  1. Twitter
    • Uses a simplistic font (Helvetica Neue)
      • Is easy on the eyes
      • Not difficult to read.
    • Color scheme (blue, white, black) is not complicated and is not too flashy.
    • Has an easy layout for a user to understand.
      • Tabs that can lead to different parts of a page
      • Is not too difficult to follow since the tab is highlighted to make sure you are clicking on the right one
      • Color changes in accordance to what tab you’ve selected like “home”, “messages”, etc.
    • Serves as a social media outlet
      • Can communicate with others around the world and under friends' tweets or media pictures
      • Can upload thoughts or feelings for others to view
      • Can see what's "trending" at current time
  2. Webtoons
    • Uses a simple font
      • Is not too harsh on the eyes
      • Comes in white, black, or green
    • Promotes almost every “original” story so users can see the art style and click on the one they’d enjoy
      • Is unlike most other comic websites like Tapas, which only shows four stories and fills the rest of the page with unrelated topics
    • Tabs are easy to use and organized by sections such as “Genres”, “Most Popular by Genre”, and “New to Webtoons”
      • For example, the website has a section that shows what story chapters are released depending on the day of the week.
      • If the tab “Saturday” is selected, it changes color, and different stories appear.
    • Allows any user to post their drawings or comics for others to enjoy
  3. Twitch
    • Utilizes the following colors: white, black, and purple.
      • A simple yet pleasing color scheme that pairs well
      • Is not harsh on the eyes
    • Font is not difficult to read
    • Text size changes depending on what video you click on
    • Doesn’t overcomplicate it by adding a lot of tabs and keeps it straightforward by adding only three at the top bar
    • If a user wants to watch a streamer while browsing for others, Twitch minimizes the current stream in the bottom left corner and allows the user to search on its site for other streamers
      • Is quite useful since most other sites force the user out of the current stream if they want to search for other categories or streamers
    • In the “Browse” section, Twitch adds the title of the categories as well as pictures so users can select whatever topic might interest them.
      • Nice to see since other sites may only place the title of the categories in a way similar to how Wikipedia formats their site
    • Can serve as a job to some
      • Streamers can get sponsorships or donations from viewers
    • Fun to interact with streamers who play a game a viewer might be interested in

Sites I Strongly Dislike and Reasons Why:

  1. Apex Legends Wiki
    • Front page is overwhelming
    • Too many red, bright boxes on front page
      • Harsh on eyes at first glance
    • Too cluttered with the site’s subsections
    • Has ads at the top that can clash with the site’s main colors
    • Side tabs need to be at the top
    • Seems to be updated by individuals who choose to update the site with any info they feel fits
      • May or may not be accurate
    • Background of site makes some words hard to see
    • Text is too small and hard to read at top bar
      • Needs to be larger
  2. Bing
    • May not be able to find whatever topic I'm searching for
      • My intended search may appear in page three
    • Front page is not appealing to look at
      • Makes me think it’s a photography site and not a search engine
    • Filled with irrelevant topics/news stories at bottom bar
    • Front page photo clashes with Bing’s text
      • Makes it difficult to read
  3. PlayStation Game Store
    • Dark background is not appealing to look at
      • Should be standard white
    • Certain texts are a tad too dark and shaded
      • Needs to be a brighter white
    • Cluttered with game images
    • Site’s sidebars need to be at the top
    • Difficult to go back to previous page once a game’s clicked on
    • On mobile, site is difficult to use
      • Mobile version of site is worse since bars are too small to be able to click on the right one
    • On PS4, store site runs slow
